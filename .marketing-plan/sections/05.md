# 5. Activation

*How a gym goes from "signed up" to "this is essential to how we run."*

## The aha moment

A gym owner becomes a retained customer the first time they see the kiosk working on their tablet with a real member walking through it — especially if that member is new and just signed the waiver without anyone at the front desk. That's the moment the product's value is visceral, not abstract.

Everything in activation is aimed at getting a new gym to that moment within their first 48 hours.

## The activation problem to solve first

Right now there's no self-serve onboarding — you walk every gym through setup. That's fine for the first 3 customers and will teach you where the friction is. But it won't scale past 10, and it blocks any self-serve acquisition you try to run. **Reducing setup-to-kiosk-live to under 2 hours, unassisted, is the most important product/marketing work of Q2.**

## Activation stages

**Stage 1: Signup (day 0)**

Ask for only three things: gym name, owner email, password. Everything else goes in setup. More questions = more drop-off before they see the product.

*Current state:* No confirmed self-serve signup path. Each gym is onboarded manually. Flag as open decision — see §13.

**Stage 2: Setup wizard (day 0–1)**

Order optimized for time-to-kiosk-live:

1. Gym name + logo (5 min)
2. Add first 5–10 members or CSV import (10–20 min) — highest friction point
3. Create one class (5 min)
4. Set up one membership plan (10 min) — billing can be skipped initially
5. **Kiosk test** — open `/kiosk` on a tablet and check in as a test member. This is the aha moment.
6. Belt requirements — optional at setup; day-2 feature, not a day-0 blocker

**Stage 3: First week email sequence**

| Day | Email | Goal |
|-----|-------|------|
| 0 | "Welcome — here's your setup checklist" | Get them through the wizard |
| 1 | "Your kiosk link is ready" | Push them to test the kiosk on a tablet |
| 3 | "Add your membership plans before your next billing cycle" | Prevent billing setup stall |
| 7 | "How's the first week going?" | Personal check-in from founder; surface blockers |
| 14 | "Your first 2 weeks — here's what to do next" | Belt progression setup, member portal intro |

The day-7 email should look like it came from you personally. Plain text, first name, direct question.

**Stage 4: Activation milestone (day 7–14)**

A gym is "activated" when they've done all three:
- [ ] At least 1 member checked in via kiosk
- [ ] At least 1 class created
- [ ] At least 1 membership plan set up

Track manually at first. Instrument properly at 10+ customers.

## Friction points to eliminate

**Member import.** Every gym switching from Mindbody or spreadsheets has a member list. A CSV import (name, email, phone, belt rank) is the single highest-impact activation feature to build.

**Payment provider setup.** Connecting Square or Stripe requires API keys. The setup wizard needs dead-simple copy with screenshots. A video walkthrough here is worth more than any polish elsewhere.

**The kiosk URL.** The last step of the setup wizard should be a big button — "Open kiosk on your tablet" — with instructions for pinning to home screen on iOS and Android.

## Assisted onboarding (first 10 gyms)

Do it yourself. But structure it:
- 30-minute Loom walkthrough sent before the call so the call is Q&A only
- Shared checklist doc the gym follows along
- WhatsApp or text for quick questions in week 1

Every friction point hit during assisted onboarding goes into the product backlog. By gym 5, you'll know exactly where the wizard breaks down.
