# 4. Acquisition

*How strangers become aware of MatConnect.*

## Channel map

| Channel | Status | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Founder's personal network | **Active now** | Highest conversion, zero cost — this closes the first 3 |
| BJJ community (Reddit, Facebook groups, Instagram) | **Active now** | Where the ICP already spends time; authentic founder story travels well here |
| Demo video (YouTube) | **Build in Q1** | Single most leveraged asset — every sales conversation links here instead of a live call |
| Direct outreach (cold DM to gym owners) | **Q2** | Once there's a case study to lead with, cold becomes warm |
| SEO / content | **Q2–Q3** | Long game; start planting seeds in Q2, harvest in Q3+ |
| Paid social (Instagram/Facebook) | **Q4 at earliest** | Not until CAC is known from organic and there's budget to test |
| PR / podcast appearances | **Q3** | BJJ podcasts exist and are receptive; needs 5+ customers first for credibility |
| Partnership with BJJ gear brands / affiliates | **Q3+** | Natural distribution but requires relationship capital first |
| Google Ads | **Skip for now** | Search volume for "BJJ gym software" is too low to justify cost before organic is validated |
| LinkedIn | **Skip** | Wrong platform — BJJ gym owners are not on LinkedIn |

## Q1 — Close the first 3 (weeks 1–12)

**Move 1: Close the warm prospect.** This is the only job that matters in week 1. Get them live, document everything about their setup process, fix every friction point. They become the case study that makes every future conversation easier.

**Move 2: The network list.** Write down every BJJ gym owner or instructor you know personally. Coaches from tournaments, people from open mats, instructors from affiliates, guys you've trained with who now run their own schools. Aim for 30 names. Message each one personally — not a mass email, a real message. The ask is not "buy my software" — it's "I built something for gyms like yours. Can I show you what it does? 15 minutes."

**Move 3: Film the demo video.** At your own gym. Show the kiosk on your actual tablet, walk through a member checking in, show a new member signing up and signing the waiver, pull up the belt progression dashboard. No script needed — you know this product cold. 3–5 minutes. Upload to YouTube unlisted first, then public. This video replaces a live demo for most prospects.

**Move 4: Show up in the community — as a practitioner.** Join the relevant Facebook groups (BJJ gym owners, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Business, etc.) and r/bjj. Don't post about MatConnect yet. Answer questions about running a gym. When someone complains about Mindbody or asks how other instructors track belt progress — that's when you mention what you built. The ratio is 90% helping, 10% mentioning the product.

**Move 5: Wire up basic analytics.** Install Plausible or GA4 on the landing page before anything else goes out. You need to know how many people visit and where they come from before spending any more time on acquisition.

## Q2 — Earn the right to go broader (months 4–6)

By month 4 you should have 3+ paying customers, at least one solid testimonial, and a live demo video. Now the acquisition work broadens.

- **Turn customer 1 into a case study.** One page. How many members, what they were using before, what changed. This becomes the first piece of content on the site and the proof point for every outreach message.
- **Start cold DMs with social proof.** Find gym owners who complain publicly about their current software on Facebook/Reddit. Open with the case study, not a pitch.
- **Instagram presence.** Post from the founder's gym — check-in kiosk in action, a belt promotion logged in the system, a screenshot of a member's progress dashboard. Authentic product-in-use content.
- **Post on r/bjj and BJJ business forums.** Tell the founder story. These posts perform well when they're genuine.

## Q3 — Build the SEO foundation (months 7–9)

**Target keywords (low competition, high intent):**
- "BJJ gym management software"
- "Brazilian jiu-jitsu member tracking"
- "belt rank tracking software BJJ"
- "gym check-in kiosk martial arts"
- "Mindbody alternative BJJ"

**Content to build:**
- 3–4 comparison posts ("MatConnect vs Mindbody for BJJ schools")
- 1–2 problem-aware posts ("How to track belt progression for 100+ members")
- YouTube: "MatConnect vs Mindbody" + tutorial videos rank in YouTube search

## Q4 — First paid experiment (months 10–12)

Only run paid once there are 10+ customers, a validated case study, and $200–500/mo available to test without needing it back immediately.

**First test:** Facebook/Instagram retargeting only — show the demo video to anyone who hit the landing page. Lowest-risk paid experiment.

**Not yet:** Broad awareness ads, Google Search, influencer sponsorships.
